vs
✓ Specific & structured
You are a science journalist writing for a general audience. Summarise in 3 bullet points the main causes of Arctic sea-ice loss since 2000, citing one key statistic per point. Avoid jargon.
Five rules of thumb
  • 1Be specific. Vague prompts get vague answers. State the exact task, audience, length, and tone.
  • 2Show, don't tell. A worked example teaches format better than describing it in words.
  • 3Assign a role. "Act as a senior data scientist" primes a more expert register than asking bare.
  • 4Ask for reasoning first. "Think step by step before answering" reduces errors on multi-step problems.
  • 5Iterate. Treat your prompt like code — test, observe the failure mode, patch one variable at a time.
